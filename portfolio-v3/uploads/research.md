To create a software developer web portfolio in 2026, you should treat it as a **"sovereign entity"** that serves as the primary interface between your human expertise and both human recruiters and machine intelligence.

Here are the translated and expanded guidelines for your portfolio:

### 1. Content: What to Include
Your portfolio must move beyond simple code lists to tell a narrative of **impact and problem-solving**.

*   **Clear Value Proposition**: Your homepage should immediately communicate who you are and the unique value you provide. Use an eye-catching headline that defines your niche (e.g., "Software Engineer building AI-powered tools that boost developer productivity by 20%").
*   **Featured Projects (3-6 pieces)**: Focus on **quality over quantity**. For each project, describe the problem solved, your technical approach, challenges overcome, and **quantifiable results**. Use the **X-Y-Z formula**: Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z].
*   **Technical Skills**: Create a dedicated section for languages, frameworks, and tools, providing an honest self-assessment of your proficiency.
*   **Humanized "About Me"**: Share your story, what motivates you, and your unique work style to help employers assess cultural fit.
*   **Architectural Evidence**: For senior roles, include **standardized architecture diagrams** (like C4 models or deployment diagrams) to showcase your strategic reasoning and trade-off decisions.
*   **Social Proof**: Incorporate testimonials and recommendations from clients or colleagues to build trust.

### 2. Design: Aesthetics and Functionality
Design in 2026 focuses on authenticity and intentionality to combat "template fatigue".

*   **Bento Grid Layout**: This modular standard organizes information (like projects and GitHub heatmaps) into a visually balanced, scanable hierarchy.
*   **Dark Mode**: This is now an expected standard for tech-savvy audiences and can also signal sustainability by saving device battery.
*   **Micro-interactions**: Use subtle animations (like button hover effects or form validation) to provide feedback and make the site feel "alive".
*   **Kinetic Typography**: Use geanimated text for hero elements to command attention, while ensuring it remains readable and respects motion preferences.
*   **Neo-Brutalisme**: This "anti-aesthetic" uses raw visuals, monospace fonts, and terminal-like interfaces to signal **personhood and authenticity** over polished AI-generated templates.

### 3. Technical Guidelines: Discoverability (GEO)
Your site must be optimized for **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** to be found by AI-powered search engines.

*   **Performance & Mobile-First**: Target a **Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)** of under 2.5 seconds and ensure perfect mobile usability.
*   **llms.txt Protocol**: Add an `llms.txt` file to your root domain. This serves as a "treasure map" for AI agents, directing them to the most relevant canonical evidence of your skills.
*   **JSON-LD Schema Markup**: Use structured data (like `Person`, `CreativeWork`, and `Review` schema) to explicitly define your attributes for search engines and LLMs.
*   **Machine-Extractable Content**: Use a **"Bottom Line Up Front" (BLUF)** approach by leading sections with 40-80 word summaries that AI agents can easily extract as "Answer Nuggets".
*   **Accessibility**: Strictly adhere to **WCAG 2.1 Level AA** standards. This includes maintaining high color contrast and respecting the `prefers-reduced-motion` system setting.
